What are calories?

Calories are a numerical unit of energy that is given to the body through the intake of food. Calories are made up of three macros.

What are macros?

Macros stand for macronutrients and are carbohydrates, fats and proteins. The three main components that our food we consume consists of.

Carbohydrates 1g of carbohydrates is equal to 4 kcal. Carbs are the main energy deliverer in our body. With this being said we have to make sure that we consume enough carbs to provide energy for our daily activities as well as our training. The amount of carbs per person can massively vary. If you stick to the average numbers of fat and protein stated below, you can use the rest of your daily calorie intake as carbs.
Fats 1g of fat is equal to 9 kcal. Fats are essential for hormones and specially important for females. Fat helps with providing structure and strenght for our cells as well as transportation of vitamins throughout our body. As an average I would set fats no lower than 0.8kg of kg body weight.
Protein 1g of protein is equal to 4 kcal. Protein helps grow and maintain all body tissue. That's not just muscle but also skin, organs, tendons and much more. Protein provides high satiety. That helps with feeling fuller for longer. As an average I would set protein to about 2g of kg body weight.
Alcohol 1g of alcohol is equal to 4 kcal. Alcohol is often referred to as the fourth macronutrient. Where as fats, proteins and carbs can be stored in the body and used for energy, alcohol can't be stored and the body simply wants to get rid of it as fast as possible. It brings zero nutritional value. This is why we also call alcohol empty calories.

How does an example calculation for macros look like?

Lets take a female, that is 167cm tall and weights 60kg. She used the calorie calculator and got given 2169 Calories.

  • Protein: 2g x 60kg = 120g of protein per day.
    120g x 4 kcal = 480 kcals worth of protein
  • Fats: 0.8g x 60kg = 48g of protein per day.
    48g x 9 kcal = 432 kcals worth of protein
  • Carbs: 2169 (daily kcal) - 480 (protein) - 432 (fats) = 1257 kcals left for carbs
    1257 / 4 kcal = 314g of carbs per day.

How do I know how many calories I'm consuming?

You track your calories. That means you keep a record of everything you eat in a day. I highly recommend using MyFitnessPal. It's an easy to use app where you can track all your calories and macros. Please be aware you are not tracking your exercise through MyFitnessPal. It will add your "burned" calories to your daily intake. Unfortnuately apps as well as sports watches highly overstimate the calories burned and you will probably end up overeating.

How do the goals refer to the calories?

If your goal is to maintain, your calories will take the following into account:

  • Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR): The amount of calories your body needs to function. This varies depending on age, height, weight and gender.
  • Non-Exercise Activity Thermogeneis (NEAT): The amount of calories your body needs for daily activities.
  • Exercise Activity Thermogenesis (EAT): The amount of calories your body needs for training.

If you want to lose weight, the calculator will but you in a deficit of 25%. If you want to build, the calculator will but you in a surplus of 20%.
